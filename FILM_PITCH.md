# The Permanent Way — Film Pitch

## Logline

When a catastrophic HS2 engineering failure accidentally tears open a portal beneath the Chilterns, Victorian titan Isambard Kingdom Brunel is resurrected. Furious, confused, and riding a stolen horse, he sets out to rebuild Britain’s broken railway network and restore the soul of the nation.

Starring Tom Cruise as Brunel.

---

## Tone

A blend of:

- Steampunk spectacle  
- Dry British humour  
- Engineering realism  
- Mythic undertones  
- Action‑adventure energy  

---

## Key Scenes

### The HS2 Breach
Night shift. Rain. A misaligned tunnel boring machine. A harmonic vibration. A fissure opens. Brunel steps through, furious.

### The Horse Theft
Brunel steals a massive Victorian‑style horse from a nearby farm and rides into the night.

### The Thames Tunnel
Marc Brunel appears as a spectral mentor, guiding Isambard through the metaphysical under‑infrastructure.

### The Clifton Suspension Bridge
Tom Cruise insists on hanging from the chains for real.

### The Beeching Office
A bureaucratic boss fight where paperwork flies like shurikens.

---

- National identity  
- Lost potential

- The power of reconnection
