# The Permanent Way — Gameplay Overview

## 1. Core Loop

The player, as Brunel, must survey, plan, negotiate, and rebuild the railway network of the United Kingdom using:

- Real‑world mapping data  
- Satellite imagery  
- LIDAR  
- Historical alignments  
- Civil engineering simulation  
- Political and social negotiation  

The game blends engineering realism with narrative spectacle.

---

## 2. Gameplay Pillars

### Engineering Simulation
A physics‑driven system inspired by BeamNG, focusing on:

- Track alignment  
- Gradients  
- Earthworks  
- Bridge loading  
- Tunnel stability  
- Drainage  
- Electrification envelopes  

Mistakes are visible, catastrophic, and educational.

### Real‑World Data Integration
Players can:

- Import OS maps  
- Overlay historical routes  
- Propose new stations  
- Simulate passenger demand  
- Generate costed proposals  
- Export documents for real‑world campaigns  

### Political & Social Friction
Players must navigate:

- Local councils  
- Environmental impact assessments  
- Landowners  
- Bus lobbies  
- Treasury budget caps  
- Consultation periods  

### Narrative Progression
Brunel encounters:

- Abandoned lines  
- Lost stations  
- Structural anomalies  
- Bureaucratic obstacles  
- Ghostly visions of Marc Brunel  

---

## 3. Opening Sequence

The game begins at the HS2 breach site. Brunel emerges, surveys the chaos, and undertakes his first mission: stabilise the breach and reconnect a severed local line.

---

## 4. Long‑Term Goals

- Rebuild the national network  
- Restore the Great Western Railway  
- Reopen the Somerset & Dorset  
- Reconnect rural communities  
- Heal the metaphysical wounds of the Permanent Way
